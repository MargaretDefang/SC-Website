SPEAKERS

We’re happy to welcome over twenty speakers to present on the industry’s latest technologies. Prepare for an inspiration extravaganza.

Chris Mills

HEAVY METAL CODING

Heavy Metal Coding is a performance-oriented development mindset that emphasizes technical rigor, disciplined architecture, and low-level optimization. Inspired by the complexity of heavy metal music, it treats code as an engineered system built to operate under demanding conditions and encourages developers to embrace complexity rather than avoid it.

About Chris

Chris Mills is a Senior Developer Advocate at Google, where he focuses on web standards and developer tools. He has over 20 years of experience in web development, having worked at Mozilla, Opera, and W3C. Chris is passionate about making the web a better place for developers and users alike.

  • @chrismills
  • chrismills.com

Estelle Weyl

Designing the Web for Everyone

Estelle Weyl advocates for a web that is accessible, inclusive, and adaptable. She believes that great web design isn’t just about aesthetics—it’s about creating experiences that work for all users, regardless of device, browser, or ability. By prioritizing accessibility and progressive enhancement, developers can craft websites that are both functional and delightful.

Her philosophy encourages experimentation within real-world constraints, balancing creativity with usability. Estelle emphasizes that understanding user needs and embracing diversity in design perspectives leads to more impactful and meaningful digital experiences.

About Estelle

Estelle Weyl is a web developer, author, and accessibility advocate. She has written extensively on web standards, HTML5, CSS, and JavaScript, and speaks regularly at conferences about building inclusive web applications. Her work combines technical expertise with a strong commitment to user-centered design.

Through teaching, writing, and mentoring, Estelle inspires developers to think beyond code and focus on the human experience of the web. Her contributions have helped shape modern web development practices, emphasizing clarity, accessibility, and maintainability.

  • @estelleweyl
  • estelleweyl.com

Shay Howe

Less Is More: How Constraints Cultivate Growth

By setting constraints, we force ourselves to focus on what truly matters. Shay Howe emphasizes that limitations—whether in time, technology, or design—can drive creativity and foster problem-solving skills. By embracing simplicity and prioritizing clarity, designers and developers can create more effective and elegant solutions.

About Shay

Shay Howe is a designer and developer who has been building websites since 2003. He is the author of the book Learn to Code HTML & CSS" and has taught web design at the School of the Art Institute of Chicago.

Throughout his career, Shay has contributed to the web development community by writing tutorials, speaking at conferences, and mentoring new designers. His work emphasizes clean, maintainable code, semantic HTML, and CSS best practices. Shay's approach combines technical proficiency with a strong sense of design aesthetics, making his teachings invaluable for both beginners and experienced developers.

He continues to inspire web professionals worldwide, showing that with focus, constraints, and iteration, anyone can create elegant, functional web experiences.

  • @shayhowe
  • learn.shayhowe.com

Erica Decker

Empowering Innovation Through Collaboration

Erica Decker believes that the most transformative technology is built when diverse minds collaborate. By fostering inclusive teams and open communication, we unlock creativity and drive solutions that no individual could achieve alone. Collaboration, she argues, is not just a strategy—it’s the catalyst for innovation.

Her approach encourages developers and designers to share knowledge freely, experiment without fear of failure, and embrace feedback as a tool for growth. Erica emphasizes that building technology is as much about people as it is about code.

About Erica

Erica Decker is a software engineer and tech leader with experience in building scalable applications and mentoring new talent in the industry. She has led engineering teams at several tech startups and frequently speaks on topics like leadership, team dynamics, and innovative software design.

Through her talks and workshops, Erica inspires professionals to focus on collaboration, continuous learning, and leveraging diverse perspectives to solve complex technical challenges. Her mission is to create tech environments where everyone can contribute, learn, and innovate.

  • @ericadecker
  • ericadecker.com
